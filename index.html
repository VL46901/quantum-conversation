The Conversation

I have a question that actually is quite simple when I think about it, but I would like to know how I get to mathematically understand.. because obviously a negative and a positive can weigh the same just on different spectrums of a weight scale, but I would like to ask. How does a neutron in a proton weigh the same?

That's an excellent question about fundamental particles!

Actually, neutrons and protons don't weigh exactly the same, though they're very close. Let me explain:

Neutron vs. Proton Mass

  • Neutron: approximately 1.675 × 10^-27 kg
  • Proton: approximately 1.673 × 10^-27 kg

The neutron is slightly heavier than the proton by about 0.1% (or 1.4 MeV/c² in energy units).
